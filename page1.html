Lamborghini

Lamborghini is an Italian luxury sports car manufacturer founded by Ferruccio Lamborghini in 1963. The company is known for producing high-performance, exotic cars that are often recognized for their sleek designs and powerful engines.

Founded in 1963 by Ferruccio Lamborghini, the Italian luxury sports car manufacturer Automobili Lamborghini is renowned for its high-performance and exotic vehicles. Key models include the groundbreaking Miura (1966), the iconic Countach (1974), and subsequent models like Diablo, Murciélago, Aventador, and Huracán. Lamborghini is celebrated for its powerful V12 engines and distinctive design philosophy featuring sharp angles and a low-slung profile. The brand's cars are synonymous with extreme performance, combining acceleration, top speeds, and handling capabilities that rival the best in the industry. The introduction of the Lamborghini Urus in 2018 marked the brand's foray into the SUV market, blending high-performance features with practicality. Lamborghini is known for limited editions, adding exclusivity to its lineup, and has changed ownership several times, currently under the Volkswagen Group, a subsidiary of Audi. Beyond vehicles, Lamborghini is regarded as works of art, symbolizing luxury and performance, and continues to push the boundaries of automotive design and engineering.
